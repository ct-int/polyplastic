Module 01

Account / Customer Module

What This Module Does

This is the starting point of the entire CRM. Every customer in your system is called an "Account" in Salesforce. The most important thing here is figuring out whether the customer is someone you already do business with, or a brand-new prospect.

If a customer is marked as Existing, it means they're already in your SAP system (the finance/operations software). Salesforce automatically pulls their data from SAP — you don't have to type it manually. The SAP Customer ID field gets filled in automatically.

If a customer is marked as Prospect, they are new — not yet in SAP. The sales team manually creates their profile in Salesforce. There is no SAP ID yet. Once they actually buy something and are formally onboarded, they'll be synced to SAP.

This separation keeps your data clean and prevents confusion between real paying customers and potential ones.

Module 02

Product Categories & Pricing Module

What This Module Does

Your company makes 6 different types of products, and each one is priced differently. Some products use a mathematical formula to calculate the price (based on size, material, quantity etc.), and some use a pre-set rate card (like a menu with fixed prices per item).

When a salesperson creates an Opportunity (a potential sale) and chooses a product category, Salesforce automatically shows the right pricing tool for that product — either a formula calculator or a rate table.

This prevents pricing mistakes and saves the salesperson time because they don't have to manually calculate prices or search for rate cards in Excel files.

Module 03

Opportunity Module – Department Input Tracking

What This Module Does

Once an Opportunity is created, it cannot go straight to a customer quote. First, 5 internal departments need to review it and give their input. Think of it like an internal approval checklist before you promise anything to the customer.

Engineering checks if it's technically possible to make. Design reviews the visual/creative requirements. Purchase confirms raw materials are available and at what cost. Testing & Validation sets quality check requirements. Packaging specifies how it will be packed and shipped.

Each department gets a notification in Salesforce when it's their turn. They fill in their inputs and mark it "Completed." Only after all 5 say Completed does the Opportunity become "Ready for Quotation."

This way, no quote is ever sent to a customer without all internal teams being aligned first.

Module 04

Quotation Module

What This Module Does

Once all departments have given their inputs and the Opportunity is "Ready for Quotation," the sales team creates a formal Quote document in Salesforce. This quote contains the final product, price, payment terms, and delivery details.

A PDF is auto-generated from the quote using a professional template. But before it can be sent to the customer, it must go through an internal 3-level approval:

Step 1 – KAM (Key Account Manager) reviews the commercial terms. If they approve, it moves to Step 2.

Step 2 – CMO (Chief Marketing Officer) checks strategic alignment. If they approve, it goes to Step 3.

Step 3 – CTO (Chief Technology Officer) validates technical accuracy. Only after CTO approval is the quote considered "Internally Approved."

If anyone rejects the quote, it goes back to the sales rep with comments, and the process restarts.

Module 05

Quote Delivery to Customer

What This Module Does

Once the quote is internally approved by all three levels, it's time to send it to the customer. The delivery method isn't fixed — it depends on what type of customer or quote content is involved.

The system looks at the "Content Type" field on the quote and automatically suggests the right delivery method:

🔹 Email — For standard quotes, the PDF is attached and sent via email directly from Salesforce.

🔹 Courier — For sensitive or large formal commercial quotes, a physical copy is sent. Salesforce creates a dispatch task for the team.

🔹 Portal — For customers using your digital portal, the quote is uploaded and they receive a notification to log in and view it.

After sending, the quote status updates to "Sent to Customer" with timestamp and sender details recorded automatically.

Module 06

Negotiation Module – Customer Visits & MOM

What This Module Does

Sending the quote doesn't mean the deal is done. The customer might want to discuss, negotiate, or request changes. This module handles that entire back-and-forth negotiation phase.

The Sales Rep books a Visit in Salesforce — either a physical meeting or an online call. After the meeting, they create a Minutes of Meeting (MOM) record that documents exactly what was discussed and agreed upon.

The MOM includes a heading (meeting title), detailed discussion points (what was talked about), and tags the internal team members who were involved. It's linked to the visit and the specific quote being negotiated.

If the customer wants changes to the quote, the system helps create a revised quote version. This cycle can repeat — visit → MOM → revise quote — until the customer is satisfied and gives final approval.

This way, every negotiation conversation is recorded and traceable, with no information lost.

Module 07

Sample Request Module

What This Module Does

After the customer approves the quote, before a full production order is placed, they typically want to see a physical sample of the product. This is common in manufacturing — you want to make sure the product looks and works exactly as specified before ordering thousands of units.

The Sales team creates a Sample Request record in Salesforce. This tracks every step: how many samples were made, when they were sent, what the customer's feedback was, whether any modifications were needed, and whether there were any quality issues.

If the customer says "looks good!" — the sample is marked as Approved and you proceed to invoicing. If they say "please change this" — you revise and re-send. Salesforce even counts how many revision rounds were needed.

If the sample is rejected entirely, it's escalated to management for a decision on how to proceed.

Module 08

Proforma Invoice (PI) & Purchase Order (PO)

What This Module Does

Once the sample is approved, it's time to talk money formally. The client issues a Proforma Invoice (PI) — think of it as a pre-invoice or a bill estimate. It tells the customer: "Here's the total amount, here's how much advance we need, and here are the payment terms."

The customer then reviews the PI and responds with a Purchase Order (PO) — an official document from their side that says "We agree to buy this and here are our internal approval details." The PO is uploaded directly into Salesforce as a document.

Together, the PI and PO form the formal commercial commitment between your company and the customer. Only after the PO is received can the actual production order be created and sent to SAP.

Module 09

Order Creation & SAP Integration

What This Module Does

When the Purchase Order is received from the customer, it's now time to create a formal Sales Order in Salesforce. This order is built from the approved quote, so all product details and pricing are already there — no re-entry needed.

The customer's PO document is uploaded to the order. Then, the order is automatically sent to SAP — your company's main ERP (enterprise resource planning) system that manages manufacturing, inventory, and finance.

SAP creates its own "Sales Order" on its side and sends back a SAP Order ID. This ID is automatically written back into the Salesforce Order record, creating a permanent link between the two systems.

Now both systems know about this order. Salesforce handles the customer relationship side; SAP handles the operational and financial side. They stay in sync via the Integration Status field which shows: Pending → Sent to SAP → SAP Order Created.

Module 10

Invoice & Payment Tracking

What This Module Does

After SAP creates the Sales Order, the finance team in SAP creates invoices against that order. These invoices are then automatically synced back to Salesforce, so the sales and account management teams can see the payment status without leaving Salesforce.

Each invoice shows the total amount billed, how much has been received from the customer, and what the outstanding balance is. The status updates automatically as payments are received in SAP — Salesforce reflects these changes in near real-time.

Sales managers can view an Accounts Receivable dashboard in Salesforce showing all customers with outstanding payments, overdue invoices, and payment trends.

If a payment is overdue, automated reminder emails are sent to the customer. The sales rep also gets a notification so they can follow up personally if needed.

This module completes the full circle — from the first customer contact to the last rupee collected.
